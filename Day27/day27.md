
# 🚀 Day 27 – The Path to Approval

## abtalks 60 Days Claude Challenge

### Understanding the Prior Authorization Journey Through Interactive Storytelling

---

# 📖 Overview

For **Day 27** of the **abtalks 60 Days Claude Challenge**, I built **"The Path to Approval"**, an interactive storytelling application using Claude.

The application explains one of the most important yet least understood healthcare workflows—the **Prior Authorization (PA) process**.

Instead of presenting complex documentation, the project follows a patient named **Rahul** through every stage of the journey, allowing users to experience the workflow in an engaging and educational way.

> **Complex systems become easier to understand when people can experience them rather than simply read about them.**

---

# 🎯 Challenge Objective

Build a complete interactive application that allows users to:

- Experience the Prior Authorization workflow
- Follow a patient journey from diagnosis to approval
- Understand medical necessity evaluation
- Learn how insurance companies review requests
- Explore denial, appeal, and approval scenarios
- Learn healthcare workflows through storytelling

# 📚 What I Learned

## 1. Healthcare Workflows Are More Complex Than They Appear

Behind every approved treatment is a structured process involving patients, providers, and insurance companies.

---

## 2. Storytelling Improves Learning

Presenting a workflow through a real patient journey makes complex topics easier to understand than static diagrams or documentation.

---

## 3. User Experience Matters

Educational applications become much more engaging when users can interact with the content instead of simply reading it.

---

## 4. AI Can Accelerate Educational Software

Claude helped transform a complex healthcare workflow into a clean, interactive learning experience.

---

# 💡 Biggest Insight

> **The best educational software doesn't overwhelm users with information—it guides them through an experience they can understand and remember.**

---

# 🌟 Final Takeaway

This challenge showed me that AI can be used not only to build applications but also to make complex real-world systems easier to understand.

By combining storytelling, interaction, and thoughtful design, even complicated healthcare workflows can become engaging learning experiences.
